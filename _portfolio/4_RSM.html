--- title: "Reflective Shadow Maps" excerpt: "Paper implementation of Reflective Shadow Maps by Carsten Dachsbacher and Marc Stamminger.
" collection: portfolio --- I implemented Reflective Shadow Maps (RSMs), a real-time rendering technique introduced by Carsten Dachsbacher and Marc Stamminger. RSMs approximate one-bounce indirect illumination, a crucial part of global illumination, by extending standard shadow maps to store additional per-pixel data (depth, position, normal, flux).

Key Features

  • Developed a Frame Buffer Object (FBO) manager to generate shadow maps with extended attributes.
  • Implemented multi-pass rendering: shadow map generation, direct lighting, low-resolution indirect lighting, and full-resolution interpolation.
  • Designed sampling patterns and jittering for improved indirect illumination quality.
  • Used OpenGL shaders to handle light projection, transformations, shadow computations, and flux calculations.

Results

  • Achieved realistic indirect illumination effects with RSMs, combining direct and indirect light contributions.
  • Rendered scenes at ~17 FPS with 400 samples, showing plausible global illumination.
